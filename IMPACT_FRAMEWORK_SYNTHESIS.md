# ENS Public Goods Term 6 - Impact Framework Synthesis

## Executive Summary

This document synthesizes deep research on all 6 strategic grantees to demonstrate verifiable impact aligned with the ENS Public Goods charter. The charter evaluates grants based on **usefulness and impact** across three core areas:

1. **Infrastructure**: Protocol improvements and developer tools for Ethereum
2. **Utility**: Projects solving real problems for Web3 users and developers
3. **Education**: Initiatives that help people understand and use Web3 technologies

---

## Strategic Grants Impact Analysis

### 1. Vyper - $50,000 USDC

**Organization**: Vyper (Ethereum smart contract language)

#### Infrastructure Impact ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

- **Protocol Contribution**: Translating ENS Universal Resolver to Vyper (3-month project, Oct 2025)

- **Ongoing Work**: In-depth fuzz testing and compilation performance improvements

#### Utility Impact ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

- **Systemic Risk Mitigation**: CVE-2025-21607 fix prevented silent failures that could have caused major exploits
- **Developer Experience**: UX improvements in v0.4.1 reduce friction for secure app development
- **Third-Party Validation**: Secured matching funds from Ethereum Foundation

#### Education Impact ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Global Workshops**: Vyper workshop at ETHTokyo (Sept 2025)
- **International Outreach**: Chinese community translations and content
- **Local Engagement**: Multiple events in India (Oct 2025)
- **Educational Partnerships**: In talks with BuidlGuidl for curriculum integration

**Key Verifiable Metrics**:
- $50,000 USDC grant + EF matching funds
- 4 security fixes deployed (March 2025)
- $4.7B TVL secured
- 1 workshop at ETHTokyo + multiple India events

**Sources**: ENS Forum, Vyper docs, GitHub releases, NVD CVE database
